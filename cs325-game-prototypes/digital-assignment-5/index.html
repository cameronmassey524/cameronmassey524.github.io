  • High Concept: Players must use weapons to maneuver an object to the end goal of the level. Resources will be limited, and players will have to think and experiment to complete the level.

Precise timing and aim can mitigate the need for these adjustments.
  • Prototype Goal: Create a system of weapons which inflict physically based effect on targets.
  • Player Experience Goals: Game experience should be that of a puzzle game that requires some amount of technical skill to execute your strategy.
  • Gameplay: Player have three weapons/tools to help them get the ball to the goal. The first weapon is a rocket, which can be leveraged for its projectile travel time and also its large explosion radius. The second weapon is an energy beam which will constantly push the ball while it is hitting it, but has limited range. The third weapon is the "quark cannon" which fires a light-speed (hitscan) "projectile" that knocks the ball in the direction it is hit. It is incredibly precise and fires instantly with no range limit, but you must allow it to cool down before firing again or changing weapons.
  • Strategies: I think that generally, a common strategy would be to launch a rocket at the ball, then guide it through the air by pushing it with the energy beam, and finally using the quark cannon for the final adjustment. Using the rocket first allows you to get into a more useful position and switch weapons while the rocket is traveling through the air. Following this with the energy beam gives you a constant way to manipulate the ball over a period of time.
